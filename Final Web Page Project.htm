How to Travel Internationally for Super Cheap!

The luxury of being able to go throughout Europe and Asia for long periods of time is just a dream for many of us but I am here to tell you how to do it cheaply and effectively. With meticulous planning, cost conscious travelers such as yourself can enjoy the history and beauty of many cities all over the world at a fraction of the price. Although backpackers on a limited budget may have to sacrifice some of the nicer amenities, they can gain invaluable experiences by going to cultural and not tourist attractions.

Checklist for your trip

  • Sturdy backpack for easy movement between destinations
  • Hostle reservations
  • Flight reservations
  • Currency for the countries you will be visiting
  • Most importantly a plan of where you will be going and what you will see

The 2 Easiest Ways to Save Money

1. Plane Flights

One of the biggest and best way to save money when starting your travels is choosing the best and cheapest plane flight for your trip. There are hundreds of different airlines and websites that show rapidly changing and varying prices across all websites. Many websites such as Priceline and Momondo offer great deals on flights all over the world but sometimes are misleading in the prices they quote you as well as how many stops the flights have which from personal experience can cause major problems. One of the websites that I like to use the most is Google Flights because it is a very easy-to-use interface that allows me to compare specific travel days with the prices of them so I know that I am finding the absolute best prices. You can also use many different types of filters on many of these websites if you prefer to fly with a specific airline or you want to fly nonstop flights only.

Another great tip is to be flexible on the days that you choose to travel, if you are flexible on the days as well as the months even that you travel then you will have a much easier time finding those flights that are super cheap and exactly what you're looking for.

2. Accommodation

The other easiest and most important way to save money when you are traveling is to search and find hostles and other cheap hotels in order to maximize the amount of money you save when you are abroad. There are a plethora if different options to choose from including Couch Surfing and Hostels International that have hostels... and couches for you to sleep on all over the world. Generally though during peak season many hostles get reserved long before your trip so I recommend that you book far in advance, usually 2-3 months if you have that much time to plan. With proper planning and excecution you too can learn to travel extremely cheaply and be able to book hostels for less than $15 per night at times.

FAQs

How do I find cheap transportation within the cities I visit?

In almost all of the cities you will visit in your time abroad will have a lot of different options for transportation including busses and trains as well as the in-city metro where you can almost always purchase passes for less than 10 euros per day which will allow you to go all round the city for that price. If there is no mass transportation provided than you can access then you should be able to rent bicycles on the street for a similar price for the entire day.

How can I make sure I am getting the best plane ticket price?

Well, that is a difficult question because the answer is that you can never really be 100% sure that you are getting the absolute best price for the plane ticket that you get. As I said in the Planes and Accommodation tab though, there are ways to ensure that the price that you get is on the low end. For domestic flights its best to book 57 days in advance and for flights to Europe for example studies have shown that the best time to buy them is about 120 days in advance.

When is the absolute cheapest part of the year to travel?

There really is no magic dates that flights will be cheapest at any point during the year but I will say that October and September are very cheap travel times as well as flights that leave and or return on Tuesdays and Wednesdays, as well as flights that leave and or return on days that the country of origin has a major holiday.
